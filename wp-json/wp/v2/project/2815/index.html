Acute diseases are those that typically develop rapidly and have a short duration, often requiring prompt medical attention. Examples include the common cold, influenza, strep throat, and appendicitis. Symptoms can vary widely depending on the specific disease but may include fever, pain, inflammation, and respiratory or gastrointestinal issues. Causes can range from viral or bacterial infections to physical trauma or allergic reactions. Diagnosis often involves medical history, physical examination, and sometimes laboratory tests.
